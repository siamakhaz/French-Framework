## ğŸ“ How to Use This Framework

### For Complete Beginners
1. Start with the **Exam Overview** to understand the test format
2. Review **Section A basics**: question types and simple responses
3. Build foundational vocabulary from the word lists
4. Practice basic conjugations daily
5. Use playbooks to see examples in context

### For Intermediate Students
1. Dive into **Section A grammar** and response frameworks
2. Expand vocabulary with thematic lists
3. Work through **Section B topics** you're comfortable with
4. Practice argumentation structures
5. Focus on fluency and natural expression

### For Advanced Students
1. Master **Section B** advanced grammar (subjunctive, conditionals)
2. Practice all 150 discussion topics
3. Work on nuance and sophistication in arguments
4. Time yourself under exam conditions
5. Focus on pronunciation and intonation

### Recommended Study Schedule

**6-8 Weeks Before Exam:**
- Study 1 hour daily: 30 min grammar, 30 min vocabulary
- Complete 2-3 playbooks per week
- Focus on Section A fundamentals

**4-6 Weeks Before Exam:**
- Study 1.5 hours daily: mixed Section A & B
- Practice speaking out loud daily
- Review 10 discussion topics per week

**2-4 Weeks Before Exam:**
- Study 2 hours daily: heavy Section B focus
- Timed practice sessions (15 min each)
- Record and review your responses

**Final Week:**
- Light review of notes and key phrases
- Practice relaxation techniques
- Review exam logistics

## ğŸ’¡ Study Tips & Best Practices

### Speaking Practice
- **Talk to yourself** in French while doing daily activities
- **Record yourself** and listen for mistakes
- **Use voice memos** to practice responses to random topics
- **Find a study partner** or language exchange

### Vocabulary Building
- Create **flashcards** for thematic vocabulary
- Use the **spaced repetition** method
- Focus on **high-frequency words** first
- Learn vocabulary **in context**, not isolation

### Grammar Mastery
- Don't just memorize rulesâ€”**practice using them**
- Create your own example sentences
- Focus on accuracy first, then speed
- Review conjugations daily (even just 5 minutes)

### Exam Preparation
- **Simulate exam conditions** regularly
- Practice with a timer
- Learn to manage nervousness
- Develop strategies for when you don't know a word
